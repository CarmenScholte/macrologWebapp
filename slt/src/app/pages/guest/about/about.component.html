About

Who are we?

Hello there! This is the Macrolog team.

Macrolog was developed by us: two software engineers who wanted to get in shape. After using several food tracking applications we got a little tired of adds and payed functions which, in our opinion, should be free. So for laughs and learning, and like every stubborn programmer, we decided to take it upon ourselves to build a better app. This hobby project got a little out of hand and what started as a simple Android app has now turned into a platform of food intake and energy expenditure analyses. Or rather, that's what we want it to be in the future. I guess we are somewhere halfway.

Why Macrolog?

We were looking for a way to track food intake based in macronutrients rather than calories. We believe that to effectively change your body composition, whether it's losing weight or building muscle, tracking macronutrient is the way. Unfortunately, in most food tracking apps calories come first and macro's come second. Sometimes entering a different ratio of your prefered macronutrient intake is a function you have to pay for. This, together with the general desire to dive into app development, is why we decided to flip these priorities and build an app that focusses on macro's first.

What we are good at

We are good at making software. We like making new features, add new divices and platforms to hook up to ours. We love gathering data about every aspect of nutrition and exercise, and we love analyzing this data to make better estimations and to slowly perfect our diet. That is what Macrolog is for, gathering data and see what you're actually doing, and see what works to achieve a physical goal.

What we are not so good at

Macrolog will probably never include dietary advice for anyone. This is because we have zero educational background in nutrition or food chemistry. We won't pretend to know much about dieting and we feel we are not in a place to tell anyone what to do. Also, the concept of the "right" proportions is somewhat debatable, and what works for one person may not work for another.

In the end, we just want to help you measure.
